The Clinical Gap

Financial tasks are among the last skills patients recover — and the hardest to practice in a clinic.

Most people leave rehab without ever practicing the exact tasks they'll face at home: logging into a bank, paying a bill online, managing a credit card. Money Rehab fills that gap.

01
No safe practice environment exists

Therapists can't have patients log into real accounts during sessions. There's no consequence-free way to practice bill pay, login flows, or account navigation — until now.

02
Financial IADLs are high-stakes at home

Missing a bill, overdrafting, or navigating a confusing portal can have real financial consequences. Patients need rehearsal before they're on their own.

03
Existing tools don't target this skill set

Generic cognitive apps don't mirror real-world financial interfaces. Money Rehab uses realistic banking and utility portals — the actual environments patients use every day.

What Makes It Work

Built around how patients actually
use financial tools at home.

Feature 01

Realistic interfaces, not simplified ones

Each module mirrors a real financial portal — a utility company website, a Visa credit card account, and a credit union banking dashboard. Patients practice the actual navigation patterns and visual layouts they'll encounter at home, not a dumbed-down approximation.

Authentic visual design Real-world navigation Multi-screen flows
Feature 02

Targets cognitive rehabilitation goals

Financial tasks demand working memory, sustained and divided attention, sequential processing, and executive function — exactly the cognitive domains most affected after stroke or TBI. Money Rehab was designed to challenge and train those skills in a functional, meaningful context.

Working memory Divided attention Executive function Sequential processing
Feature 03

Zero setup. Ready in seconds.

No downloads, no accounts, no software installation. Open a browser on any device — laptop, tablet, or desktop in your clinic — and you're ready. Patients can also use it independently at home on any device with a browser. Nothing to configure between sessions.

Browser-based Any device Approved access list Home use ready
Feature 04

Safe environment, real consequences removed

Everything is simulated. No real accounts are ever accessed, no real payments are processed, and no personal financial information is entered or stored. Patients can make mistakes, get confused, and try again — exactly the kind of low-stakes rehearsal that builds confidence and skill before they're managing real money independently.

No real accounts No data collected Unlimited attempts

Money Rehab FAQ

Financial IADL, online banking, and bill pay practice FAQs.

Answers for occupational therapists, speech-language pathologists, neuro rehab teams, educators, caregivers, and approved users.

What is STRIVE Money Rehab for occupational therapy and speech therapy?

STRIVE Money Rehab is a browser-based financial IADL and functional cognition simulator for occupational therapy, speech therapy, neurorehabilitation, stroke recovery, and brain injury rehabilitation. It gives approved users realistic practice with online banking, bill pay, credit card account review, and everyday money-management tasks.

Is STRIVE Money Rehab appropriate for patients with cognitive impairment?

Yes. The simulated bill pay, online banking, and credit card tasks can support treatment goals for attention, working memory, sequencing, problem solving, executive function, and financial IADL retraining. The bill pay module is often a good starting point, while online banking tasks are more demanding.

Can patients use Money Rehab independently at home between therapy sessions?

Yes, after approval. STRIVE Money Rehab runs in a modern browser, but access is limited to emails that have submitted the agreement and been manually added to the approved short list. Clinicians and caregivers can then use the simulations for home practice and carryover.

Do patients enter real banking or credit card information?

No. STRIVE Money Rehab uses simulated financial tasks and does not require real account numbers, payment details, passwords, or banking credentials. The practice environments look realistic, but they are fictional and contained within the browser.

What devices work best for online banking and bill pay practice?

STRIVE Money Rehab runs in modern browsers on desktop, laptop, and tablet devices. A laptop or desktop is recommended for realistic keyboard, mouse, and screen navigation practice, especially when targeting computer use, online banking, and bill pay skills.

How can clinicians document STRIVE Money Rehab in therapy notes?

Clinicians may document STRIVE Money Rehab under financial IADL training, functional cognition, executive function, computer use training, cognitive rehabilitation, problem solving, attention, sequencing, and self-monitoring goals depending on the plan of care and patient performance.
